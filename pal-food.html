OVERVIEW

Personalized Active Learner (PAL) is a closed-loop, contextually-aware, personalized and user-centric wearable device which leverages Artificial Intelligence and Biotechnology for Memory Augmentation, Language Learning, and Behavior Change.

 

PAL aims to enable people to design their lives (i.e. inspire behavior change) to optimize their cognitive, physical, and emotional well-being, while providing a holistic view of their daily activities and physiology. People's actions deeply influence their internal and external bodily states, which in turn affect people's actions. PAL aims to help people to be aware of the correlations between their different activities and internal states so that better behavioral awareness can drive intrinsically motivated behavior change. For example, how a user’s sleeping, eating, social or meditation patterns correlate with their physiology signals , their hormones and how does it impact epigenetic changes - this helps the user to be self-aware and consciously choose the best activities for themselves.

 

PAL-Food an automated body manager solution for all those who hate manually writing into an app all the foods they eat throughout the day. PAL-Food is a wearable system that can automatically tells us the number of calories we consume and burn throughout the day. This wearable developed combines information from three sensors : magnetometer, camera and epidermal glucose sensor  to calculate calorie intake. PAL-Food can effortlessly get connected to the smart-phone so we can see whether we need more or less food, water , exercise , sleep or relaxation. Above all we can listen to our gut talk which gives us a feedback on what we eat. A device like PAL-Food could aid in helping us face ourselves, control obesity, diabetes and stress eating.

 

The wearable, developed uses three off-the-shelf sensors blocks to detect in real time the type of food, the amount of food consumed , and a continuous monitoring of Glucose through sweat using epidermal sensors. The blocks are as follows:

PAL- On Device real time Food Detection : Automatic food detection systems can help us to process food-intake estimation and dietary assessment. However, due to the nature of food images, their recognition is a particularly challenging task on a miniaturized wearable device like PAL. By fusing the Mobilenet and Yolo Darknet model a custom architecture was built using Tensorflow to produce good accuracy and speed. This architecture was tuned on a food recognition dataset containing 9435 × 512 pixel images of 100 different classes of food  on which a classification accuracy of 83.72%, along with an accuracy of 91.72% on a detection dataset containing 1,753 images. The model was deployed on a raspberry pi zero as a part of the PAL device making it easily wearable.

Image Credits: UEC FOOD 100 Dataset

Epidermal Glucose sensor: Biofeedback is an important ingredient for most of the wearable healthcare devices. Considering Calorie estimation, continuous Glucose monitoring is a prime biofeedback to estimate the amount of calories consumed and burnt. A non-invasive approach was necessary in order to make it easy to wear not compromising on the reliability of the sensor data, hence developed an epidermal tattoo which can monitor the sweat glucose levels. The development of the sensor from scratch included micro-fabrication technology, electro-chemistry, development of Bluetooth Interface Modules to send the data to a smart-phone,  signal processing, circuit design and flexible PCB fabrication. An android application is being developed to collect the glucose data from the sensor. We are looking forward to collect data from subjects using this setup.

Chewing detection using miniature magnets: Intake monitoring was the next step as it plays an important role in preventing and treating many healthcare problems  including obesity and diabetes. A new novel approach was developed in order to track the amount of food a person is chewing/ consuming. A miniature magnet is tagged along the persons ear/ jaw and the change in magnetic flux due to the jaw movement could be tracked using a magnetometer coupled with the PAL device. The magnetometer data can further be classified to differentiate activities like chewing, talking, coughing etc using time series data analysis. Accurate eating recognition using the above method could be the basis for automatic dietary monitoring and can help trigger other sensing API's like the  the PAL wearable camera could be triggered when the eating recognition system detects chewing of food; a personalized digital food journal of the person, which includes time and duration of eating and pictures of food, can be generated and nutrition analysis could be done.

Connecting the dots , the epidermal glucose sensor helps in continuous glucose monitoring of the person using a non invasive approach, the chewing detection system accurately recognizes the activity of eating and triggers the food detection API on the PAL device
