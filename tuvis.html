TUVIS: AI and augmented reality experimental Storytelling

BACKGROUND : “Access to e-learning and commerce still remains a distant dream for many in India, and this project is an artistic device to draw attention to it”.

OVERVIEW

​Digital inequality denies children’s education, women’s livelihoods, and entire communities the opportunity to build better-informed lives. Whether it is the lack of technology itself or the skills to use it effectively and safely; poverty and social exclusion inevitably follow. The concept is the brainchild of Ananadana Kapur (ODL Fellow 2017 – 2018), who is founder-director CINEMAD India, an award-winning filmmaker and communications designer she combines film with social change initiatives and works in a consultative capacity with UNDP, UNICEF, and IGNCA. I am the Technology Co-Creator and Co-Principal Investigator for the initiative leading the Technology and design Teams to achieve Creative AI for social good.

 

Tuvi’s/E-Devi’s Core Experimentation for a Mobile Centric Story-telling journey was little by little. The initial experiments revolved around artificial intelligence, combining computer vision and automated machine learning on a curated list of documentary Photographs that were abstract and depicted the Digital Divide. The experiments were streamlined to Quantitate sentiments, creativity, identifying shapes, gestures, or objects. As tech-savvy and advanced artificial intelligence sounds, it didn't seem to humanize elements that could translate to an interesting experience. It was too artificial or banal, which led to an exploration of Poetry which remains a very human trait since ages. The Idea of Dynamic storytelling based on the sentiment trend online is something that caught the teams’ eye which helped in prototyping Co-creative AI-Human Poetry/Story scripting which could further be matched with the visual semantics of the Theme-based  Documentary Images collected, thereby leading to a scripto-visual experience.

In addition to the above experiments on Scripto-Visual Storytelling with AI, Open AI’s GPT & Computer vision the experience had to be participatory, educative & driven by Curiosity. For which we chose Components of Augmented Reality Experience, 3D Art, and Trending Filters for the user to participate in privacy preserved AR Experience to take Oaths for a more Equitable  & Safe Digital World and also experience the metaphorical Digital Storyteller(The Goddess)  in Augmented-Reality.

Facial recognition, advanced Object Detection, and AI Components of the experiment were not supported by small, lower quality smartphones. This led to a final challenge, where the platform must work seamlessly anytime, anywhere on most smartphones with a constrained hardware environment. Adapting the experience to different mobiles meant designing multiple paths, and not burning the phones. The Prototypes developed led to a Universal Browser-based experience that could be run even on a hardware-constrained device.

 

With the Prototype in place, the Co-creative Team did try to address and experiment with the Experiential Questions like Should the Story cards consist of a blank canvas, visual poems, AI-Generated Depth effects, and more importantly should a narrative be constructed with a beginning, a middle and an end? If so How? Could we add dynamic Visual context to the story with the audience participation? Would a cryptic AI-Human Poetry bring Curiosity and motivate the audience to participate?

The last tip point of the Prototyping! With the ever-increasing reach of Social-networks and the heated debates on Personal data, we at TUVIS meant anonymizing the content experienced by the users in Augmented Reality with Artistic Filters and Pledge banners masking their identity.

In Short Prototyping Tasks :

  • Dataset Creation with accumulating Documentary Photographs which emote the Digital Divide and Other Abstract Images
  • AI & Computer Vision for Visual Semantic reasoning, Creativity and Object detection as a Block to see how the Curated Dataset is Understood by machines
  • Textual Content from Content Creators :
  • NLP Techniques to Train and Fill up textual Completions for given poetic phrases
  • Matching AI & Human Co-Created Script with Visuals (Using metric Scoring Mechanism )
  • WebXR : Augmented Reality Based participatory Story-telling for the audience to participate , learn and Experience a Visual Digital Form of the Story-Teller (The Goddess)
  • Stitching up the Scripto-Visual-audio Storytelling Framework
