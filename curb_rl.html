Behavioral Nudges for Responsible Curb Space Utilization

Offline Reinforcement Learning Models to reward adherence to rule based curb utilization.  Counterfactual Causal analysis on parking violations & Traffic congestion data

OVERVIEW

Mobility (Freight & Private Vehicles)  systems regularly suffer from an imbalance of parking space supply within the city, leading to users being unable to receive services. It is observed that these imbalances lead to users not getting access to services or users committing violations which could intern affect the balance of the city leading to congestion and reduction in safety. Recently, there is an increasing interest in applications of reinforcement learning techniques for improving the resource supply balance and service resilience of the systems. In the current context, I have employed RL to achieve the goal of producing an effective user incentivisation policy scheme to encourage users of parking spaces to slightly alter their travel behavior in exchange for a small monetary and moral incentive(sustainable credits). The recommended slight changes in user behavior are intended to over time, increase the service level of parking spaces, reduce violations, and thereby the chain reactive effect of congestion. It also leads to improving the user experience of urban spaces leading to a more sustainable and equitable city. In the current efforts, I have been exploring three important questions :

 

  1. What State-action representations should be used to produce an effective user incentive scheme for utilizing parking spaces
  2. How do parking violations affect congestion and thereby lead to carbon foot-print, helping us index behavior to Carbon Credit incentives
  3. How effective are reinforcement learning-based solutions on the balancing problem under varying levels of resource supply, user-demand, violations & Congestion correlations, and carbon footprint reduction?
