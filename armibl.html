OVERVIEW

As a part of the team building the startup nexrea we are looking forward to build the next generation reality for education and Healthcare sector. In collaboration with Dr.Paul Kim from Stanford who developed SMILE(Stanford Mobile Inquiry based learning System) we are building an Augmented Reality Inquiry based learning system for helping students learn science.

 

 The potential use of mobile devices has attracted many educators and researchers to conduct inquiry based learning (IBL) activities in a classroom or a fieldwork. As a team we are looking to utilize , augmented reality (AR) technology which has become widespread for conducting learning activities using mobile devices. In the literature related to science education, there are limited mobile systems found in which AR technology is used for IBL activities. We have therefore designed a mobile system MAPILS to conduct plant IBL activities using AR technology. Further, two application-related aspects of SMILE(Stanford Mobile Inquiry-based Learning Environment) evaluation framework are used.

 

Inquiry-Based Learning (IBL): IBL is a pedagogical approach in which learners tend to gain knowledge about the underlying domain through exploration and investigation. This type of learning activity can be used in different domains such as arts, mathematics, sciences and so on. However, in the sciences, IBL activity is designed with a purpose to assist students in order to understand the natural phenomena by conducting experiments, collecting data and interpreting results to generate explanations . In an IBL activity, a suitable level of support is required from inquiry-based instructions provided by the systems. There are four types of inquiry-based instructions including confirmatory, structured, guided and open by which students can engage in a typical IBL activity. In confirmatory inquiry, learners are asked to perform an inquiry by giving a problem (question) and the description about a procedure in order to comprehend previously introduced knowledge in the classroom. In a similar fashion, learners tend to perform IBL activity with known question and procedure in structured inquiry. However, in this inquiry, learners are challenged to understand the unknown phenomena by interacting with the environment. On the other hand, guided inquiry is different to structure inquiry in such a way that in this inquiry learners need to design their own procedure to explain the natural phenomena. In open inquiry, learners are challenged to investigate the underlying problem by devising their own procedure, finding their own problems and collecting data in their own way. This free exploration form of inquiry may create heavy load on learners’ memory while performing inquiry investigations. Thus, in this research, we have used guided inquiry, which is more challenging than confirmatory and structured inquiries

 

 

In consultation with CMS and SMILE, we have chosen one of the topics “of Projectile Motion” from science book as the experimental context to evaluate the the AR system. The system was developed using Vfuria Unity SDK 5.1.1 (see https://developer.vuforia.com/downloads/sdk) that supports the augmented reality library to present virtual objects. Blender 2.7 was also used to design 3D models of the plants.

 

The system consists of three main modules; learning, training and testing. The learning module presents information regarding projectile Motion and Kinematics with a AR game using a ball. Students have to use their mobile devices to target a particular marker and play with the projectile motion game with a virtual ball. The information will be displayed on their mobile devices once the student selects any part of the projectile track. After selecting a specific part of the track then the information will appear about that velocity, acceleration direction etc. Also audio will be played if students require. On the other hand, testing module is designed that evaluates the learning outcome of each student what they learnt during the learning module. This system asks randomized questions related to different concepts related to Kinematics. For example, a randomized question is like: “Identify the direction vector of the Ball at time t- 5 sec”.  Students can take first five minutes to understand the concepts behind augmented reality and how this application can be used. The rationale of designing this training module is to briefly explain such kind of systems in advance because they all are new to the students.
