Bike Re-balancing in  Cities Optimization

Offline Reinforcement Learning Models for optimization and rebalancing  bikes across stations in a city

OVERVIEW

 

The Current Bike transit systems in cities give ease of access to micro-mobility bikes for customers where they can simply pick up a bike from one station, rent it for a certain period of time and cost, and subsequently return to another station in the network. Most of the Bike utilization is for short-duration trips, and this dynamic nature of the system may lead to situations where bike shortages occur in locations with high demand, and on the other hand, bike surpluses accumulate in locations with low demand.

 

Balancing the Supply demand of bikes spatiotemporally becomes a key customer service for the bike transit operators. To reduce the potential imbalances within the bike-sharing systems, the operators can look up to two potential solutions as follows:

  1. Incentivisation schemes for Bike users to re-balance the stations
  2.  Additional operators to redistribute bicycles using trucks

 

These solutions could potentially help in re-balancing the supply-demand ratios across stations and alleviate shortages and surpluses attracting more riders while minimizing inefficiencies.

 

As a part of the  Second case, mixed-integer optimization was used to generate a strategy to meet the dynamics of the fluctuating demands of customers, while the constraint was minimizing the estimated costs of operations for re-balancing. The employment of mixed-integer techniques helped create the best set of decisions to find the optimal schedule, a number of bikes to re-balance to minimize the objective function (Operational Cost), and also satisfy other constraints imposed on the system such as the number of bikes a truck can carry at one time, the capacity of the bike docking stations, etc.

For Case 1, a Reinforcement learning frameworks were used to strategise incentivization schemes for users and change their behaviors
