Representation Learning for Mobility

Representational Learning:  Generating embeddings for spatial regions using mobility, crime & Other demographic data.

OVERVIEW

Urban data troves have been increasing exponentially enabling us to understand urban dynamics, assist planning in making cities more livable and sustainable. With the large trove of data studying quantitative representations of urban regions can help us better explore the correlations of urban properties and provide valuable insights into the structures and dynamics of cities. In order to build evidence-based systems for decision making and policy in Urban planning, it becomes important to build quantitative representations of Urban Spaces.

 

One of the biggest factors behind the recent success in ML has been the use of representations learned by deep neural networks. In particular, this ranges from text embeddings learned via LSTMs or Transformers to image representations obtained via CNNs. A common idea across these methods is to map each entity (or object) whose representation is being learned, to a single vector embedding.

In addition to image and textual representations, network embedding has also gained more attention in recent years. The low dimensional node to vector representations in networks could help us in a myriad of graph mining tasks such as node classification, community detection, and link prediction. Inspired by these techniques a representational space for Urban cities becomes easily evident, but the vector embeddings for single-layer fall short to address the complexity of real-world urban systems which give rise to a variety of networks presented in a multi-layered fashion.  Despite the widespread use of multi-layered networks, it remains a daunting task to learn vector representations of different types of nodes due to the bewildering combination of both within-layer connections and cross-layer network dependencies.

 

To address a representation learning framework for multi-layer complex networks in Urban Spaces, I have been investigating multi-view/layered joint learning models helping us model both within-layer connections and cross-layer network dependencies to learn an embedding space from urban data for urban regions. The effort consists of three steps, where the first step is to model different types of region correlations based on both human mobility and urban region properties(POI’s, socio-economic, demographics etc) . In the second step, we apply graph attention mechanisms in learning region representations from each view of the built correlation. A joint learning module is also introduced to boost the region embedding learning by sharing cross-view information. In the last step, the learned embeddings are used for downstream applications like mobility flow, usage patterns, POI Domination, land usage classification, and crime prediction in urban areas with real-world data.
