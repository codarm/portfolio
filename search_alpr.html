Search-Space reduction for License Plate Re-identification using  Domain Knowledge

 We provide an overview over the methods that use ontologies to compute similarity and incorporate them in machine learning methods; in particular, we outline how semantic similarity measures and ontology embeddings for LPR can exploit the background knowledge and how ontologies can provide constraints that improve machine learning models.

OVERVIEW

Proposed Feature Extraction for License Plate Re-identification  consists of  1) License Plate Numbers Alone ( which are vectorized using the t2nn Model) lacks any further Heterogeneous features for Classification and fine-tuned Limited Search Arena. These Heterogeneous Features could be useful at a large scale from a Universal ALPR Perspective to increase the efficiency and scalability of the approach.

 

Ontologies & knowledge graphs have long been employed in the life sciences to represent and reason over domain knowledge formally and they are employed in almost every major biological database. They are further being used to provide background knowledge in similarity-based analysis and machine learning models. The methods employed to combine ontologies and machine learning are still novel and actively being developed.

 

We propose a novel method that uses ontologies for License Plate Numbers to compute similarity in reduced search space and incorporate them in pre-existing re-identification methods; The major purpose is to understand how semantic similarity measures and ontology embeddings can exploit the background knowledge in ontologies and how ontologies can provide constraints that improve search-space models. The additional features which are being investigated to define the ontologies in the context of ALPR are 1) Vehicle Make, Model 2) Vehicle Color 3) Camera (Lat, Long) 4) Trend Analysis of State Plates at every camera/Tolling Location Time Stamp Correlations.

 

In Summary: Ontology embeddings are structure-preserving maps from ontologies into vector spaces and provide an important method for utilizing ontologies in machine learning and similarity search.

Embeddings can preserve different structures in ontologies, including their graph structures, syntactic regularities of their model-theoretic semantics. • Axioms in ontologies, in particular those involving negation, can be used as constraints in optimization and machine learning to reduce the search space.
