Magnetalk- Ever wanted to know what a person desired to say before he even speaks ?

MagneTalk enables users to talk without needing a voice using magnets and artificial intelligence,  with some interesting applications ranging from healthcare to daily routine settings.

OVERVIEW

With the era of wireless sensors and cyborgs  around, there is a  need to interface and integrate them meaningfully into our lives in the most seamless ways as possible. Here, we present Magnetalk: the silent speech using Magnetic field . Being able to recognize simple vowels without the need to speak it out, can help the speechless and paralyzed in tremendous ways and change the way we human interact with technology. The motivation and focus is on Human-Machine Symbiosis.  This project is primarily designed to recognize vowels and also help patients convey remote messages of pain wirelessly. People afflicted with facial paralysis, patients in certain medical settings unable to convey their needs or even those with minute stutter can undergo numerous hurdles in communicating in their daily lives. Silent speech can upgrade their quality of life to a great extent. In other environments, simple sentences or commands could be freely exchanged between two individuals or human and machine.

The Idea:  User's intention to speak  is characterized by the facial muscular movements  and these speech articulators are captured by the Magnetalk system using small mini magnets hooked on to the facial muscles   through feature selection on muscular areas of interest .

 

Magnetalk uses 3-axis magnetometers to measure the movement of the small magnets glued to the facial muscles. The system allows us to do speech recognition without the user having to produce any sound, i.e. Silent Speech Recognition. Recognition relies on training AI Models to differentiate the silent speech to predict vowels. The magnetometers are situated on one of two devices: a custom made around the ear earphone or a shirt hook up device as show in the picture below. The Movements of the facial muscles lead to the change in the position of magnets and hence the flux which is sensed by using the magnetometer sensor which is later calibrated and processed to predict the speech.

Data Collection: The data was collected and processed on the dragonbaord. The data is smoothed using a Gaussian filter, segmented into individual samples, and labeled. For segmentation, an automatic sliding window variance-based scheme is used to detect silences and separate them from utterances. Magnetalk system contains 3-axis magnetometers resulting in 3 dimensional time series data. However, the magnet’s movement exists in a lower dimensional subspace. So We apply PCA/ ICA dimensionality reduction to reduce model complexity by reducing the number of parameters needed to be estimated. Data set was collected for vowels alone from 5 subjects using our system and was annotated. The following data was used to train a time series CNN model to classify the silent speech into 5 classes i.e. vowels.  We achieved an accuracy of 88% for classifying the vowels.

Future Work: There are multiple directions in which this work can be extended. With our continuing data collection effort, we are targeting word and phoneme level recognition. It will be interesting to determine how much of the data required for continuous speech detection is available through just the motion of facial muscles and change in magnetic flux.

With the support from my Professor at Harvard we are also considering the capability

of developing magnetic cosmetics which can replace the miniature magnets and an epidermal magnetometer which can make the enter usage of the system very easy and have seamless interface.
