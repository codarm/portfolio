Reinforcement Learning for Sustainable Agriculture

Studying the factors using causal analysis methods for quantifying the effects of agricultural practices in reducing nutrient loss from agricultural fields and developing an RL framework by using counter-factual reasoning

OVERVIEW

Designing recommendation systems being the first step for efforts at MITTI, we wanted to understand and estimate the effects of agricultural conservation practices on reducing nutrient loss. It is also important to optimize the fertilizers utilization, irrigation systems, and other efforts for improved efficiency. Traditional, fixed or manual agricultural practices/schedules have been widely used by smallholder farmers in India and most of these strategies involve applying fixed amounts and outdated steps based on time intervals. These manual practices lead to water loss, unnecessary soil fertilizer utilization, crop burning, etc eventually leading to a reduction in crop productivity. With the evolving space of IoT, remote and ground sensing data new evidence-based strategies are being studied. MITTI’s mobile soil sensing system Quantsoil also the first version of data-based system employs strategies using thresholds or simplistic models for recommending fertilizers for smallholder farmers which might be sometimes inaccurate or non-optimal events. In addition, for sensor-based agriculture practices, an expert is needed to interpret the data and convert the quantitative metrics to appropriate threshold values for use with a scheduling model. This process is tedious needs skill labor, and is complicated considering the number of zones, rapidly changing weather, soil variability, crop types, and different water needs at various growth stages. Agriculture is a very time-sensitive process and the bulky sensor data makes scheduling events in real-time more challenging due to possibly contradictory data from different types of sensors and data sources.

 

Effective Knowledge Systems with predictive modeling are used to extract useful information from the vast trove of data and assign recommendations, However, these systems still require manual oversight to analyze and manually control the practices/recommendations. A reinforcement learning-based control method can achieve fully automatic optimal or near-optimal control. At MITTI I have been working on designing a reinforcement learning-based systems approach for controlling soil nutrient loss and improving crop yield. Using the approach for example one of the control recommendations we determine is the amount of irrigation and fertilizer for each zone at each time step, taking soil moisture, NPK Values, evapotranspiration, precipitation probability, and crop growth stage into consideration. These systems in a data-rich environment can handle much greater state space and a good number of choices. But considering the real-world scenarios in emerging economies and rural and digitally unmapped geographies, it becomes difficult to design RL systems as the counterfactuals are not answered due to scarce and messy data.

Due to messy and scarce data, the stakeholders or systems do not have full knowledge of the confounding factors, and the predictive models or conventional statistic methods can be misleading for the decision making. To solve this issue we have also been looking at using causal analysis methods for quantifying the effect of water and soil conservation practices in reducing soil nutrient loss.
