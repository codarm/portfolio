LET'S TALK

Intro:

If I had to choose a metaphor for my life's goals, it would be to loosen, if not cut open the proverbial 'Gordian Knot.' I believe there are many 'wicked' problems that plague society today that are intertwined and demand to be addressed together. To be successful in addressing them, in my own way, besides being a socio-technologist, I have also been a researcher, a social-policy entrepreneur, a chronicler, and a co-creator/artist. In this journey a question that nags in the back of my mind is the distributional consequences of technology-enabled decision systems in the society leading to a ‘Cantillon Effect’- the early recipients of data-rich decisions will benefit more significantly than those it trickles down to. Through research, my motivation is to design algorithmic interventions that are ethical, accountable, and scale social impact accounting for this complex interplay, overcoming constraints in resource poor environments.  I am currently a Budding Scientist at Conduent Labs (erstwhile Xerox Research).

Research:

Drawing inspiration from my experiences and the Machine Learning for Development  road-map,  my work can be categorized into three essential work-streams for bringing ML solutions for underserved communities: the Information Architecture & Data Reliability: stream attempts to build solutions centered on new forms of intelligent data generation, sharing, and systems learning from messy data in resource-poor settings for various domains; Deployable ML: stream attends to build Representational, Continual, Transferable and Resource-efficient Knowledge Systems which are deployable in the developing settings; and Decision Systems and Contextual Dynamics: stream looks at understanding the local context for the proposed solution to be aligned with the available means and expertise thereby creating equitable change and help stakeholders make decisions that have a larger impact.

 

Information Architecture & Data Reliability: Given that limited resources are often constraints in the developing world, It becomes critical to prioritize "How" and "What" data should be collected in the local context. In addition, adverse conditions and lack of technical expertise lead to multiple sources of biased and incomplete data converse to what ML researchers expect. Some of my work comprises of (a)  developing low-cost smartphone microscopes for point of care diagnostics, (b) Effective conversational toolkits for data gathering & sharing for the next billion users,  (c) Using interactive & immersive lightweight digital tools for geo-spatial survey, and (d) Multi-Source data integration for solving the Mobility data conundrum.

 

Along with my collaborators at MIT TATA Centre, AIIMS-Delhi, Other Government Transportation organizations these projects are conceptualized from the perspective of a socio-technical system to cover both technology and the large-scale system that surrounds it. This work has generated provisional patents and publications. Presently, projects on sustainable agriculture and Healthcare are being translated into field implementation via start ups.

 

Deployable ML:  In order to build deployable Machine learning toolkits with the data gathered in previous workstream the work in this stream spans across  Learning representations of entities across the scarce-data modalities that effectively captures their meaning, Continual transfer of the knowledge gained from learning and optimizing on past tasks (or objectives), while being robust to settings that might be adversarial and doing all of this in a resource-efficient manner.

 

Decision systems and Contextual Dynamics: I believe the previous streams are pillars for Knowledge Creation to understand socio-economic & human dynamics hierarchically. This steam helps in translating this knowledge to developing decision-making tools that help in building resilience, Equity, & trust across communities. Using Reinforcement learning techniques I have been working on planning effective strategies for mobility management , urban spaces utility and agricultural practices. To build effective decision systems  a significant bottleneck is defining the counterfactuals in a given problem context. In certain scenarios the unavailability of labeled data adds to the challenge of confounding. Such problems receive limited attention and create bottlenecks to design methods for counterfactual decision-making, thereby leaving the policy open-ended. I look forward to working on such scenarios to define  better policies and decision recommendations.
